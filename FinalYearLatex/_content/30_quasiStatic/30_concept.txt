The core idea of Quasi-Static locomotion is that the centre of mass (CoM) 
remains over the support polygon (SP) for all time. This is achieved using
the FKM, Inverse Kinematic Model (IKM), Trajectory Generation and Numerical Optimisation. \\
For both the 2D and the 3D model the same numerical optimisation functions were used. 
Equation(\ref{eq:2D_opt_step}) is recursively optimised while one foot is fixed and the other moves forward to the 
next step. During this phase, the FKM and IKM control the movement of the end effector, that being the foot in motion.
%% ARG MIN
\begin{equation}\label{eq:2D_opt_step}
    \mathbf{q}^{*} = \underset{\mathbf{q}}{\mathrm{arg\:min}}\:\:
    \mathbf{K}_{q}     \lVert  {\mathbf{q_{0}} - \mathbf{q}}    \rVert \: + \:
    \mathbf{K}_{e}     \lVert  {k(\mathbf{q}) - \mathbf{x}_{e}} \rVert \: + \:
    \mathbf{K}_{CoM}   \lVert  {\mathbf{r}^{0}_{CoM} - \mathbf{r}^{0}_{\text{Fixed}} } \rVert
\end{equation}
\\ Equation(\ref{eq:2D_opt_shift}) is recursively optimised during the double support phase, moving the CoM from the
trailing foot/support polygon, to the forward foot. Throughout this phase the CoM remains over the
support polygon. During this phase the FKM and IKM form a parallel kinematic chain, with both controlling
the mid waist.
%% ARG MIN
\begin{equation}\label{eq:2D_opt_shift}
        \mathbf{q}^{*} = \underset{\mathbf{q}}{\mathrm{arg\:min}}\:\:
        \mathbf{K}_{q}     \lVert  {\mathbf{q_{0}} - \mathbf{q}}   \rVert \: + \:
        \mathbf{K}_{e}     \lVert  {k(\mathbf{q})}                 \rVert \: + \:
        \mathbf{K}_{CoM}   \lVert  {\mathbf{r}^{0}_{CoM} - \mathbf{r}^{0}_{\text{Forward foot}} } \rVert
\end{equation}
Thus, during this phase the FKM takes the form in equation(\ref{eq:2D_parallel}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:2D_parallel}
    \mathbf{k}(\mathbf{q}) = 
        \mathbf{A}^{0}_{E_{L}}\mathbf{T}^{E_{L}}_{\text{MidWaist}}(\mathbf{q}) - 
        \mathbf{A}^{0}_{E_{R}}\mathbf{T}^{E_{R}}_{\text{MidWaist}}(\mathbf{q})
        = \mathbf{0}
\end{equation}
